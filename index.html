Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007⁴. He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu and studied physics and aerospace engineering⁴. Dr. Kalam was in charge of creating and putting the Agni and Prithvi missiles into operation¹. He was given the moniker "Missile Man" in recognition of his groundbreaking work in the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technologies¹. Dr. Kalam was also known for his utmost simplicity and humanity³. He was a true gentleman, known for his knowledge and kindness³.
